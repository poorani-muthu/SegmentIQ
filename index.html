1

Data Collection & Exploration Pandas · NumPy

Online Retail II dataset — 97,064 rows, ~4,000 customers, 10 countries, 12-month period (Dec 2010–Dec 2011). Initial EDA revealed right-skewed distributions in Frequency and Monetary, ~1% missing Customer IDs, and ~2% cancellation invoices. Exploratory histograms confirmed outlier presence requiring transformation.

2

Data Cleaning Pandas

Four-step pipeline: (1) Drop null Customer IDs — cannot be attributed to any customer, biases RFM. (2) Remove cancellation invoices (C prefix) — inflate frequency without revenue. (3) Remove Quantity ≤ 0 or Price ≤ 0 — data entry errors. (4) Drop exact duplicates. Every step logged with before/after row counts.

3

RFM Feature Engineering Pandas · GroupBy

Snapshot date = max(InvoiceDate) + 1 day. Recency = days since last purchase (lower = better). Frequency = unique invoices. Monetary = total spend. AOV = Monetary ÷ Frequency. Purchase span added for cohort context. All computed at customer-level via GroupBy aggregations.

4

Feature Scaling Log1p · StandardScaler

Monetary and Frequency are heavily right-skewed. Log1p compresses the tail — prevents VIP outliers from dominating Euclidean distances in K-Means. After log transform, StandardScaler centres and scales to unit variance. Both steps are essential for distance-based clustering.

5

Optimal K Selection Elbow · Silhouette · Davies-Bouldin

Three methods in parallel. Elbow (Inertia) shows diminishing returns. Silhouette Score (−1 to +1) measures cohesion vs separation — primary criterion. Davies-Bouldin Index (lower = better) as cross-check. Final K = peak Silhouette, cross-validated against elbow shape.

6

K-Means Clustering Scikit-learn · n_init=10

n_init=10 (best of 10 random restarts), random_state=42. Chosen over DBSCAN (too many noise points) and Hierarchical (expensive at 4K customers). RFM clusters are expected to be spherical and compact — K-Means assumptions hold.

7

PCA Dimensionality Reduction PCA · 3 components

PC1 ≈ 82% variance (Monetary + Frequency axis). PC2 ≈ 14% (Recency axis). Together 96% — virtually lossless. Enables 2D and 3D visualisation without meaningful information loss. UMAP considered but PCA is more interpretable for 3-feature input.

8

Segment Naming & Business Interpretation RFM Quartile Scoring

Labels via RFM quartile thresholds. Champion = Recency ≤ median AND Frequency ≥ 75th pct AND Monetary ≥ 75th pct. Industry-standard nomenclature. Each segment receives CLV outlook, risk rating, and 4 actionable business recommendations with estimated financial impact.
